title: Old Letters

I found your letters in a box,
tied neatly, brown with age,
the ink now pale as winter light
on every yellowed page.

You wrote of ordinary things —
the weather, bread, the rain —
yet every ordinary word
contained a world of pain.

How strange that paper holds the dead,
their voices, dry and thin,
still calling through the years to us
who never wrote them in.
