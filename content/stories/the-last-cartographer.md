In a city where all the maps had been burned, one old man still remembered the shape of the streets.

His name was Aldric, and he had spent forty years walking every lane, alley, and cobbled passage of the city of Vorheim before the Decree of Forgetting had come and made such knowledge dangerous. The authorities had decided, with the cold logic of fearful men, that a city unmapped was a city uncontrolled — and an uncontrolled city was a free one.

Every atlas, every surveyor's chart, every hand-drawn sketch on the back of a merchant's ledger had been collected and fed to the great bonfire in the square.

But they had not thought to burn Aldric.

He sat now in his narrow room above the tanner's shop, drawing what he remembered on the inside of brown paper bags — the only material no one thought to confiscate. He drew in pencil so faint it could be rubbed away at a moment's notice. He kept the bags folded in a wooden crate labelled *Old Rags*, because no soldier ever looked twice at old rags.

One evening, a young woman appeared at his door. She was perhaps twenty, with the dust of the eastern roads on her boots and a look in her eyes that he recognized immediately — the look of someone who had been lost for a very long time.

"They say you remember," she said.

He did not deny it.

"I am trying to find the Street of Weavers," she said. "My grandmother lived there. Before."

Aldric was quiet for a long moment. Then he reached into the crate and unfolded a bag.

"Sit down," he said. "I will show you."

She sat across the table from him in the lamplight, and he spread the paper bag between them, and traced with his old finger the routes she would need — through the Tanners' Quarter, across the bridge that no longer had a name, left at the ruins of the old fountain, then down a lane so narrow two people could not walk abreast.

"You must memorize it," he told her. "You must not write it down."

She nodded, leaning close, her lips moving silently as she committed each turn to memory.

When she left an hour later, the paper bag was back in the crate.

But somewhere in the city, a map was walking.
